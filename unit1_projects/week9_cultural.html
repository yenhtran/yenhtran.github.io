Getting the Answers: Asking the right questions

It's the evening before I start Dev Bootcamp and as I approach this blog, I've realized that I have a hard time asking for help. The idea of posting my idiot question online where everyone can see is most terrifying. Yes, I NEED TO GET OVER THIS and will likely be one of the biggest challenge I have to get over during these 9 weeks.

So before I start asking berating people with questions, I thought the reading material How to ask a good question was particularly useful and enlightenting.

Some great tips:

  • Write a title that summarizes the specific problem.
    • Pretend you're talking to a busy colleague.
    • Spelling, grammer and punctuation are important!
    • Write the title last! (You're have a better title.)
  • Introduce the problem before you post your code.
  • Help others reproduce the problem.
  • Include all relavant tags.
  • Proof-read before posting!
  • Post the question and respond to feedback.
  • Look for help asking for help.

Look out! It's about to get real.
